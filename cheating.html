Why should I not cheat?

From a purely utilitarian perspective, it is a fantastic idea to cheat. If I cheat smartly in a way that I do not get caught, I can increase my points. By increasing my points, I will be eligible to win.

This thinking can be extended to almost all areas of life. If I cheat at my job such as by taking credit for other's work, I can get quicker promotions; if I cheat in my relationships, I can get more pleasure(?); and so on.

Unfortunately, every cheating instance breaks my self-concept. Ego and self-concept are fluid things instead of something written in stone. There is a constant feedback loop between my actions and my self-concept. If I cheat a few times because I didn't have enough time, my self-concept will slowly start shifting into I am "a person who is lazy" or I am "a person who does not know how to manage my time" or into I am "a person who can not understand tough concepts" or I am "dumb".

There is another reason people cheat: anti-authoritarianism. I may believe that I am really smart and I will assert my smartness by showing that I can fool the organizers and get away with cheating. To begin with as organizers, We believe that you are in all likelihood smarter than we are. We are already convinced. You are brilliant.

But the problem is such external validations, including encouraging words, grades, and degrees, do not last one for their whole life. You won the competition but have you built the lasting confidence?

What actually lasts and serves in the whole life is (1) knowledge: I really deeply know what I am talking about; (2) learning ability: if I do not understand something, I have the ability to spend enough time and effort to learn really tough concepts; and (3) integrity: no matter what are the circumstances, I do not bend on my strong will and principles.

Cheating does not build either of these. You will always look out for ways to lie and fool other people, desperately trying to look smart or faithful, and can never keep lasting confidence.

However, if you build integrity, Your actions and self-concept will come in a positive loop and you will start building lasting self-confidence. If you continue this approach, you will succeed in all areas of life – personal and professional – and become a strong leader.
